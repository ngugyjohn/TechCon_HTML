History of Techcon

TechCon was founded in 2000 to bring together technology enthusiasts and professionals from around the world. Over the years, it has grown into one of the most anticipated technology conferences, featuring keynote speakers, breakout sessions, and networking opportunities.

Our Mission

The mission of TechCon is to foster innovation, share knowledge, and create opportunities for collaboration in the tech industry. We aim to inspire attendees to push the boundaries of technology and drive positive change in their respective fields.

Notable Past Speakers

Elon Musk

Elon Musk, born June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa, is a business magnate, industrial designer, and engineer. He is the founder, CEO, and chief engineer of SpaceX; early investor, CEO, and product architect of Tesla, Inc.; founder of The Boring Company; co-founder of Neuralink; and co-founder and initial co-chairman of OpenAI. Musk's contributions to the tech industry are numerous. He revolutionized the electric vehicle market with Tesla, advanced space technology with SpaceX by developing reusable rockets, and promoted renewable energy solutions through SolarCity. His work on Neuralink aims to merge the human brain with artificial intelligence, while OpenAI focuses on ensuring that AI benefits all of humanity.

Satya Nadella

Satya Nadella, born August 19, 1967, in Hyderabad, India, is the CEO of Microsoft. He joined Microsoft in 1992 and rose through the ranks to become the company's CEO in 2014. Nadella holds a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from Mangalore University, a master's degree in computer science from the University of Wisconsinâ€“Milwaukee, and an MBA from the University of Chicago. Under Nadella's leadership, Microsoft has transformed into a cloud computing powerhouse. He spearheaded the development of Azure, the company's cloud computing platform, and has been a driving force behind Microsoft's shift to a mobile-first, cloud-first strategy. Nadella's focus on innovation and collaboration has led to significant advancements in AI, quantum computing, and augmented reality.

Sheryl Sandberg

Sheryl Sandberg, born August 28, 1969, in Washington, D.C., is an American business executive, billionaire, and philanthropist. She is the COO of Facebook (now Meta Platforms, Inc.), a position she has held since 2008. Sandberg holds a bachelor's degree in economics from Harvard University and an MBA from Harvard Business School. Sandberg played a pivotal role in turning Facebook into a profitable company through her expertise in advertising and monetization. She is also known for her advocacy of women's leadership and empowerment, highlighted in her bestselling book, "Lean In: Women, Work, and the Will to Lead." Her initiatives have significantly impacted how companies approach gender equality and workplace culture.
